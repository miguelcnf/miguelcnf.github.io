What do you think DevOps is?

The simple question that almost no one gets right at interviews.

I've done my share of technical interviews for ops positions over the last year and one question almost no candidate gets right is:

What do you think DevOps is?

I mean, don't get me wrong, I am not one of those guys who expects you to answer with the exact same words I'd use to answer that myself. But, let's face it, DevOps definitions are everywhere on the internet and if you're applying for a job in a web company you should at least have a grasp of what it is.

I might fail to express where my frustration is coming from but I've heard things like:

DevOps is using chef or puppet.
DevOps are those guys who do everything.

And read things like:

DevOps is devs deploying without ops.

Obviously I still try and see what strengths the candidate has to offer, but one of those simple lame answers will always stick in the back of my mind when I'm looking back at the interview.

If I got asked that question during an interview, my answer would simply be:

DevOps is the culture of collaboration and agility between dev and ops.

My definition is as good as any but it focus on the single thing that everyone tend to agree when it comes to DevOps: collaboration is essential to achieve amazing things in the web.

There are a gazillion of other definitions and blog posts out there who touch this topic, one of the greatest is Stephen Nelson-Smith's What Is This Devops Thing, Anyway? guest post on Patrick Debois's Just Enough Developed Infrastructure blog.

In the end, I want to hang all day next to people who share my culture and mentality when it comes to getting things done and there's nothing worse then the old that's not my job fictional boundary that lack of collaboration enables.

Also it helps you not to shoot yourself in the foot, which is nice.
